Stag beetle

The European stag beetle is a species of scarab beetle belonging to the family Lucanidae. It is the largest beetle in Europe. Adults are seen from mid-June to mid-August, with June being the month with the highest number of observations.

The oldest known fossil of the group is Juraesalus from the late Middle Jurassic (Callovian) Daohugou Beds of Inner Mongolia, China. While initially interpreted as a member of Aesalinae, it was later interpreted to be a basal member of the family.

The Lucanidae males have large antlers. Their size often varies among individuals. Such variation is termed a scaling relationship or static allometry. Environmental conditions and genetic factors affect antler size.
